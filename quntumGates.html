Quantum Gates Visualization

Quantum gates are the building blocks of quantum circuits, analogous to classical logic gates in conventional digital circuits. Unlike classical gates, quantum gates are reversible and operate on qubits through unitary transformations. This page visualizes common quantum gates for 1, 2, and 3 qubits, showing their matrix representations, circuit symbols, and effects on quantum states.

Key concepts:

  • Unitary operation: Quantum gates must be reversible and preserve the norm of the state vector
  • Qubit states: Represented as |0⟩ and |1⟩, with superposition states α|0⟩ + β|1⟩
  • Tensor product: Multi-qubit gates operate on composite states using tensor products

Deutsch Gate

Universal 3-qubit gate for quantum computation with parameter θ.
•―――
    •
       D(θ)
8×8 Parameterized Matrix
Diagonal elements: 1 (except last two)
Last two diagonal elements: cos(θ), -cos(θ)
Off-diagonal elements: ±sin(θ) in specific positions

When θ = π/2: becomes Toffoli gate
When θ = π/4: creates quantum superposition
D(θ)|000⟩ = |000⟩
D(θ)|110⟩ = cos(θ)|110⟩ + sin(θ)|111⟩
D(θ)|111⟩ = -sin(θ)|110⟩ + cos(θ)|111⟩
Other states unchanged
